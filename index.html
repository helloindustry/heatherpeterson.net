When a musician plays an instrument from an internal posture of peace and groundedness, generosity and vitality, simply for joy and love those energies are transferred into the air, and the melodies carry hope and healing on their waves, profoundly impacting all who hear.

Music in its essence is a portal to hearts, and is, simply put, love in sound.

Heather Peterson

Heather is a passionate and accomplished classical pianist who masterfully blends the highest level of technique with a fundamental expression of humanity, bringing profound depth, peace, and maturity to her performances and to her teaching.

Originally a native of Oak Park, Illinois, Heather began her life as a pianist at five, beginning her lessons with W. Perry Johnson, a young teacher from Chicago who eventually settled in Oak Park. She quickly became proficient and proved a diligent student, learning the bulk of standard piano repertoire in her early years, entering competitions frequently, and performing at studio recitals often, as well as giving solo recitals as the years progressed. When she was fourteen her family moved further west of Chicago to St. Charles, Illinois. Heather began lessons with Don Walker, a piano professor at Northern Illinois University in DeKalb, maintaining her frequent participation in competitions and giving solo recitals, while picking up teaching and accompanying for income. Following a brief stint as a music business major at Anderson University in Indiana, Heather returned to NIU in 1997 to study piano again with Don Walker while teaching in the community school of music, graduating in 2001 with a bachelors degree in performance. Her interest in studying with Elizabeth Pridonoff brought her to the College-Conservatory of Music at the University of Cincinnati, where she received her master's degree in piano performance in 2003, again finding work teaching at the conservatory community school of music while there.

Shortly after graduation, Heather and her husband Nathan moved to Peoria, Illinois, to pursue songwriting with their bandmates and ministering to the community through music as pastoral staff at a local church. Heather quickly secured a position as a long-term substitute at a local elementary school teaching general music. After completing a year at the grade school, Heather began looking for other freelance work, finding she was not fully suited for the elementary general music classroom. She began working as the accompanist for Bill and Margaret Swain with the professional opera company Opera Illinois, and was also offered a teaching position at Illinois Central College as a private piano instructor. During her years as a piano professor at ICC, Heather taught class piano, gave lecture courses in music appreciation, and accompanied college choirs and recitals. She found work at a local private K-8 school as well, teaching beginning piano lessons to children before and after school. For the next ten years Heather and Nathan played weekly at their local church, and continued their creative work together in their rock band Hello Industry, writing, recording, and performing their music for large events and conferences all over the midwest. Heather moved her teaching studio to her home as she and her husband began raising a family. As her children entered the school-age years, Heather volunteered at their small private school, teaching Kindergarten and 1st grade general music for three years, and eventually directing the school choir for two years as well, supplying the school with a wonderfully rich classical music education program. She continued to teach private piano lessons from her home studio, holding student recitals and studio classes, and creating regular opportunities for her piano students to develop accompanying skills by collaborating with a local violin studio and performing joint piano-and-violin student recitals.

The years following Olivias death were clouded by grief and uncertainty. Financially, spiritually, psychologically, and physically, life felt unstable. Nathan and Heather welcomed their youngest child into their family, and slowly but steadily their days showed signs of life. On her journey through grief to healing and recovery, Heather rediscovered joy in a familiar medium: playing the piano. This time, however, it was not about achievement, perfection, performance; it was music for musics sake, and it was about expressing deep feelings and newfound understanding of the true essence of life. Heathers grief journey was aided by retraining her fingers to move again after years of hiatus, traveling through the piano pieces of her earlier studies, and allowing her deepest heart and fragile humanity to inhabit those tones for the first time.

In 2020, after a recent exploration into composition, Heather gave a full-length concert sharing all original works in various classical mediums, interspersed with her original poetry and prose. Her compositions echoed stylistic signatures of the romantic waltz, rhapsodic songs-without-words, and even the experimental 20th-century approaches to rhythm, modality, and harmonic method in an abstract tone-poem. Rounding out the program, Heather shared two songs, one of which was entitled Love Song for Livie, a moving lullaby depicting the pledge of devotion from mother to baby.

2021 marked a return to classical piano performance for Heather, as she prepared and presented a program on six separate occasions as part of her healing work and response to her experience with child-loss. Motivated by a renewed sense of purpose and vitality, she presented a revisitation of four of the most virtuosically stellar and joy-filled pieces she had studied in her former years as a college student. Wrapping up the year with a dramatically rapid move, Nathan and Heather relocated their family to the Chicago area after 18 years in Peoria, surprisingly finding themselves in a historically rich and vibrant arts community: Oak Park.

Coming full-circle with a return to her childhood homeland, Heather began to rekindle old connections and revisit her roots. After settling into their new home, the Peterson children were enrolled in the very same area schools that Heather herself had attended as a tripping school-girl. Heather eagerly jumped into freelance accompanying work at the high school and middle schools, and opened her private teaching studio in January of 2022.

Currently sharing her latest concert program in the Chicago area, Heathers playing brings joy, peace, healing, hope, and inspiration to listeners in the wake of the pandemic, trauma, and grief.

Heather holds a master's degree in piano performance from the University of Cincinnati College-Conservatory of Music, and a bachelor's degree in piano from Northern Illinois University. She has been teaching private piano lessons from her own studio for over 25 years and has held music, piano and choral teaching positions at Illinois Central College, Peoria Academy, Aletheia Classical Academy, and professional accompanying positions at Opera Illinois, and Morton Community Chorus, and Oak Park and River Forest High School.

Heather lives in Oak Park with her husband Nathan, and their four amazing musical children.
